how media ruined my self-image

as a child

i didn't want to be

korean asian different myself

This stemmed from being the only, if not, one of very few East-Asians in my rural community. On a daily basis, I was called out and tormented for my Asian facial features at elementary school. That isolation, paired with the lack of representation of Asians in the media extremely distorted my self-worth.

not once

did i think i was

or could be

beautiful

pretty

remotely

attractive

I always thought I would be thrown off to the side, that I would be cast off as “undateable” because of my ethnicity. Because I wasn’t white.

but where did that come from

?

I read a ton of books when I was little — my room had piles and piles of them. For scale, my third-grade teacher once listed, out loud, each of my classmate’s names, and how many books each kid read for that month. Most read one, or two in those 30 days. I read twenty. Multiply that from months to years —

that's

a lot of books

It’s astounding that from my entire childhood, I only remember two book series with slightly significant Asian representation in the plot. One of them was Cho Chang from “Harry Potter”. I was ecstatic that a character like her existed, and here’s why:

for once

there was an Asian who was supposed to be the “pretty one”, and not just the “shy, quiet, and smart one”.

for once

the reader is supposed to see an Asian as a character that was admired by the main protagonist.

The fact that an Asian character was portrayed like this was amazing and rare to me, so much that I didn’t even care that she didn’t end up to be that favorable of a character. I reread those books frequently, with a glimmer of hope that perhaps I have a chance like that.

Maybe that’s shallow but what can you say I was only seven

but it wasn't enough

i hated my ethnicity

As a small child, I never felt important compared to my white peers because of the lack of representation in the media and my physical environment. I never felt socially accepted.

To give a sense of how upset I was, I remember long nights of crying over not being white. There was nothing more I wanted than to have a normal name, and normal physical features.

This is what happens when you lack a childhood with an exposure to role models in your ethnic group.

of course

being older and attending a university where a solid percentage of the student body are minorities made me less reliant on media, appearance, and more accepting of who I am. I’m much happier with myself. On the whole, however, my experience with the lack of Asian representation in my neighborhood and the media might be more (or less) extreme than those of the other Asian-Americans growing up in the States. Or maybe they dealt with it better. I’m not sure

I’m not asking for pity. I only hope that reading my personal experience puts into a solid perspective of how important it is to have representation like this, no matter the minority. A more-than-decent portion of the United States is made up of them — the media should be the same. This imbalance is certainly alleviating, but there is so much improvement that needs to be made.
